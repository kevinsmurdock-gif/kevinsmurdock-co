Builder Philosophy

Build the Thing That Needs to Exist.

Every company Kevin has founded solves a problem that someone else said couldn't be solved, shouldn't be built, or wasn't worth doing. He built them anyway.

When COVID hit and no commercial lab in South Carolina was FDA-validated to run tests, we built that lab — in weeks, not months. When every pathology group was selling reps access to 50,000-name lists but nobody was giving them a platform to actually close deals, we built one. When AI voice agents were toys, we built production-grade ones for real healthcare workflows.

The pattern is the same every time: find the workflow that's broken, pay attention to the people doing the work, then build the thing that should exist — infrastructure first, polish last. Ship fast. Fix it live. Listen more than you sell.

Twenty-five years, five states, 450 employees at the peak, 25+ companies. One builder.

Builder's Stack

Five Things That Haven't Changed in 25 Years

  1. 01
    Build in Greenville, SC.
    Every company. Every team. Every hire. There are great people here — and better quality of life than any coastal hub offers. No plans to move.
  2. 02
    Infrastructure first, polish last.
    Working code beats a pretty pitch deck. Ship the lab that runs PCR before the lobby gets marble. Ship the rep dashboard before it has perfect animations.
  3. 03
    Pay attention to the people doing the work.
    Reps know what reps need. PhD scientists know what the lab needs. The boss doesn't invent product — the boss clears the path so the team can.
  4. 04
    Share the upside.
    Millions reinvested into expanding capacity and hiring workers who lost jobs during shutdowns. Not a PR stunt — an operating principle. When the company wins, everyone wins.
  5. 05
    Show up when it counts.
    Free tests for every SC first responder. Thousands of N95s donated. Hiring laid-off workers mid-pandemic. You don't get called a "valuable partner for South Carolina" by a US Senator for a press release — you get there by showing up.
